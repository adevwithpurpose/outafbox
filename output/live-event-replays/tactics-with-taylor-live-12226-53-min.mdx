Tactics with Taylor Live 1/22/26 (53 min)

Live Event Replays

Watch as CTC CEO Taylor Holiday answers members' questions on creative production, channel expansion, and the future of ecommerce.

Timestamps:

00:00:00 – AI Creative Strategy & Workflow

Creative Systems

: Discussion of CTC's formalized creative system, Campfire, which combines initial AI image generation with human editing to produce batches of 100–200 ads.

The Subjectivity Hurdle

: The primary challenge in creative production is the "subjective endpoint" (human approval), which makes building automated systems difficult.

Brand Specificity

: Successful AI creative requires an "intimate level" of interaction with each brand to understand specific sensitivities, such as scenery realism or font accuracy.

Process Transparency

: Taylor suggests a future walkthrough of the workflow to help clients understand how historical prompts and brand standards are layered into the AI process.

00:03:50 – Ads Manager Updates & Creative Diversity

Yield Curves

: Meta is increasingly adopting an interface similar to Google, providing "yield curves" that illustrate how budget increases might impact volume and cost.

Creative Diversity Reports

: Brands can request PDFs from Meta reps showing creative fatigue and freshness metrics.

Fatigue vs. Performance

: Meta data shows that

top-performing ads are often the most fatigued

; fatigue indicates an opportunity for supplemental growth rather than a mandate to turn off the ad.

Refresh Cadence

: For the example brand shown, it took approximately 14 days to reach medium fatigue and 26 days to reach high fatigue.

00:09:15 – Ad Planning & Proprietary Metrics

Core Metrics

: Taylor highlights five key metrics for ad planning:

Zero Spend Rate

,

Ad Concentration

,

ROAS/Spend Degradation

, and

Evergreen Share

.

Evergreen Share

: Defined as the percentage of ads running for more than 30 days, which provides predictability for future performance.

Budget Flexibility

: Meta now allows daily spend to range above the set budget to capture opportunities, a step toward Meta taking more control over campaign levers.

00:13:15 – State of E-commerce (January 2026)

Macro Trends

: Pure-play D2C growth is currently "grindy" for mature businesses. Growth is largely coming from international expansion, new products, and channel expansion rather than spending more on existing channels.

D2C Index Data

: Current data shows that while revenue and spend are up (10–15% and 15–18% respectively), efficiency is down.

New Customer Acquisition

: The efficiency of new customer acquisition has declined by approximately 15% year-over-year.

Market Saturation

: As markets become efficient, supply outpaces growth, leading to higher competition and less efficient growth.

00:20:25 – The Creator Market & TikTok Shop

Leverage Shift

: Early adopters benefited from a lack of market demand for creators. Now, with thousands of brands competing for the same creators, leverage has flipped to the creators, driving up costs.

Viral Content Misconception

: Taylor warns that there is often

no relationship

between viral organic content and content that actually sells services or products.

00:30:20 – Creative Supply Chain Management

Competitive Accountability

: Brands should treat creative sources like a supply chain, letting different agencies or creators compete for spend on a percentage-of-spend basis.

Deferring Risk

: Instead of paying high upfront fees, Taylor suggests setting up campaigns with cost controls and letting creators drop in as many ads as they want.

Compensation Models

: A suggested model is offering a 10% commission on spend that meets a specific efficiency target.

00:35:40 – Channel Expansion Strategy

When to Expand

: Expansion should happen as a "seed stage investment" from savings, not as a desperate reaction to a dying primary channel.

Scale-in Testing

: Use "scale-in" tests to measure incrementality in specific geographic regions where there is currently zero spend in the new channel.

High-Volume Alternatives

:

YouTube

and

Applovin

are recommended because they offer significant volume and reach unique demographics (e.g., older female demographics on Applovin).

00:44:10 – Modeling Creative Output Needs

Calculating Ad Volume

: The number of new ads needed is calculated by identifying the "gap" between desired spend and the projected spend from existing ads based on their degradation rates.

Incrementality Benchmarks

: The standard benchmark factor for 7-day click optimization is

1.2

(a 20% delta), while 1-day view often over-reports with a factor closer to

0.8

.
