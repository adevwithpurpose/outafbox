Creative Jam 10/23/25 (52 min)

Live Event Replays

CTC Creative Strategist Ben Liebman answers members' creative questions and helps ideate new creative directions.

Timestamps:

[00:00:00 – 00:06:00]

Jackson Hole Buffalo Meat

:

Gift Box Creative Review

Tammy presents new photo shoot assets focused on gift boxes and bundles.

Discussion around stop-motion “box build” animation versus traditional unboxing.

Suggestions:

Add human elements (hands, holding product).

Create multiple hooks within the first three seconds.

Vary ad aesthetics using different backgrounds and cropping.

Mix lifestyle and white-background product shots for variety.

Reminder that Meta may group similar product silhouettes together — creative diversity is key.

[00:06:00 – 00:17:00]

RING BEAR

Partnership Ad Review

David shares performance of UGC-style partnership videos.

Problem: repetitive, scripted content feels inauthentic.

Recommendations:

Let creators use their own words — remove strict scripts.

Introduce multiple hooks and overlays (“wedding ring hack,” “my wedding ring story”).

Segment videos by single value proposition (comfort fit, engraving, options).

Keep captions persistent for sound-off viewing.

Experiment with longer, more conversational content — wedding audiences are highly engaged.

Discussion on balancing whitelisted organic feel with direct-response clarity.

Richard notes: creative diversity comes from giving creators freedom to riff within brand parameters.

[00:17:00 – 00:26:50]

Bearaby

:

Weighted Blankets & Sheets Launch

Rishya discusses underperforming launch ads due to lack of creative variety.

Ben explains:

Don’t oversaturate feeds with identical product silhouettes.

Reduce repetitive imagery; create new angles and crops.

Maintain brand polish but expand visual diversity (different rooms, lighting, zoom levels).

Lo-fi (low production) UGC content can balance brand identity and testing volume.

Lo-fi = quick, phone-shot content that feels natural and authentic, not overproduced.

Example shown: Ondo socks’ simple iPhone video became top performer by sparking organic conversation.

Key takeaway: capture spontaneous, low-effort content to fill creative gaps between large shoots.

[00:26:50 – 00:31:10]

Tammy’s Jerky & Protein Snack Discussion

Brainstorming new lo-fi and UGC angles for jerky products.

Ideas:

Situational lifestyle placements (tackle box, hiking, gym bag).

Highlight protein content and nutritional angles (CrossFit or macro-focused consumers).

Capture authentic testimonials from real users about protein convenience.

Emphasis on visually aligning creator, message, and audience (CrossFit example).

Meta algorithm benefits from variety in hooks, visuals, and personas.

[00:31:10 – 00:43:45]

Matt’s Supplement Brand Launch Strategy

Matt introduces a new smoothie powder supplement (protein + fruits/veggies).

Discussion on starting from zero and building creative testing roadmap.

Ben’s guidance:

Positioning:

Lead with problem-solution (“no blender needed,” “clean on-the-go nutrition”).

Creative Strategy:

Test authentic UGC-style demos, taste tests, and “aha moment” visuals.

Show convenience — quick mixing, minimal equipment, portability.

Use “us vs. them” contrasts (old smoothie method vs. your product).

Audience Approach:

Start niche (fitness, busy professionals, women doing yoga/Pilates) before going broad.

Production:

Use iPhone content early; save budget for product validation.

Messaging:

Highlight time saved, simplicity, clean ingredients, good taste.

Engagement Hooks:

Taste-test challenges, “make a smoothie in 5 seconds” demos, authentic reactions.

[00:43:45 – End]

Closing Thoughts

Encourage balance between polished brand assets and lo-fi diversity.

Authentic creator-led storytelling continues to outperform scripted campaigns.

Brands should embrace creative discomfort — giving creators more freedom feels risky but drives better results.

Next steps: test volume-driven UGC, prioritize varied hooks, and analyze by audience segment.
