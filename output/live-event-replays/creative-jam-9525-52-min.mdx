Creative Jam 9/5/25 (52 min)

Live Event Replays

CTC Creative Strategist Ben Liebman helps ADmission members think through angles, audiences and creative production tactics.

👉

Maximizing Video Volume Deck

Timestamps/Summary:

[00:00:00–00:08:00] Creative volume & audience targeting

Discussion opens with how to scale creative output.

Advice: start by mapping key audiences (pregnancy, natural solutions, older demographics).

Creative diversity means not just visuals but also messaging, copy, and audience angles .

[00:08:00–00:12:00] Preparing for Black Friday

Focus on reusing best-performing UGC with seasonal overlays.

Build both core product/offer ads and reskinned evergreen ads.

Strategy: throw multiple variations into testing due to the short window .

[00:12:00–00:20:00] Message reframing & creative enablement

Review of “infinite meta ads” framework: reframe one asset into many.

Tactics: strong hooks in first 3 seconds, overlays with product/offer, reading reviews to camera.

Emphasis on overlays, subtitles, and maximizing low-cost variations .

[00:20:00–00:30:00] Dance Masterclass creative review

Brand presents ad tests (AI UGC, urgency messaging, trust signals).

Feedback: videos outperform images; AI UGC lacks authenticity.

Recommendation: prioritize genuine UGC, highlight differentiators, keep offers clear .

[00:30:00–00:40:00] Jewelry brand critique

Brand showcases tarnish-resistant vs sterling silver collections.

Feedback: make “tarnish resistant” the hero message; clarify branding terms.

Push collection ads/carousels (“10 gifts for Christmas”), highlight product depth, create urgency (e.g., “will sell out”) .

[00:40:00–00:48:00] Review-reading ad formats

Discussion of ad library examples.

Formats: direct-to-camera reading reviews, podcast-style with overlays, or UGC paired with product shots.

Key: low production value works if authenticity and clarity are high

Emphasis on testing diverse UGC approaches.

Authenticity, clear differentiators, and product detail shots are critical.

Quality bar is lower now; relatability and variation matter more than polish .
