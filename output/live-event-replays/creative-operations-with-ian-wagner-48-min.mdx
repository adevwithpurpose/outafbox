Creative Operations with Ian Wagner (48 min)

Live Event Replays

Join Ian Wagner, who heads up CTC's creative operations for branded creative, as he walks through his approach to creative volume.

Timestamps:

Overview of Creative Operations

Role and Focus

: Ian Wagner manages creative operations at Common Thread Collective (CTC), specializing in producing branded ads at scale.

Core Objective

: The primary challenge is scaling to thousands of ads without compromising brand integrity or causing team burnout.

Art vs. Systems

: While creating a single ad is relatively straightforward, reaching high volume requires transitioning from seeing an ad as a "work of art" to viewing it as a "creative system".

Ad Differentiation

: CTC utilizes two creative paths: UGC (User-Generated Content) and "branded ads," which are defined as ads that are not UGC.

Four Core Principles for Scaling Creative

Creative Frameworks

: These frameworks should clearly define the purpose of an ad and remain flexible enough to support the team when they lack new ideas.

The "Yes And" Mindset

: Borrowing from improvisational skills, this principle encourages trying new creative angles—like founder videos—to meet different audiences rather than strictly locking down brand ideas.

Full Feedback Loops

: It is essential to create a system where feedback from the end of the production process is stored and carried back to the beginning to inform future briefs.

Team Quality

: Building a great team is identified as the most critical factor in choosing between constant struggle and high-level success.

Tactical Execution & Strategy

Persona/Pain Point Multiplier

: By identifying three personas and three pain points per persona, a team can quickly generate nine distinct ad angles for a single product.

Filling the Sponge

: Branded ads are designed to "fill the sponge" by building exposure and trust, while performance ads "squeeze the sponge" to generate revenue.

Platform Native Variance

: To prevent Meta’s system from flagging ads as duplicates, designers must "shake up the ingredients" by varying headlines, layouts, and product scales so that every ad looks different when squinting.

The Production & Briefing Process

The Recipe Concept

: Each ad is treated as a "recipe," where the brief includes the product, destination URL, layout ideas, specific assets, and copy for the designer.

AI-Assisted Research

: AI is used to scrape customer reviews and landing pages to automatically generate personas and pain points, ensuring the team doesn't start with a blank page.

Global Talent Management

: Ian hires designers from platforms like Seek (specifically in the Philippines) and allows them to work in their own time zones to maintain a happy, productive team.

Closing Q&A: Managing Creative Roles

Evolving Skills

: As AI tools like Gemini and Nano Banana improve, the designer's role is shifting toward the ability to prompt and direct specific details like camera angles, lighting, and model attributes.

Hiring a Manager

: A Creative Operations Manager should be a strong project manager with "people skills" who can sustain systems and prove delivery on creative goals.
