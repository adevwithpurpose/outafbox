Tactics with Taylor Live 12/19/25 (47 min)

Live Event Replays

CTC CEO Taylor Holiday answers ADmission members questions on 2026 predictions, BFCM postmortem, and building relational equity with content creators.

Timestamps:

Black Friday–Cyber Monday performance & macro context

[00:00:00–00:02:05]

Overview of BFCM performance across brands: median YoY growth around 9–10%, with most gains driven by price/AOV rather than unit volume. Order volume declined, BNPL usage increased, and pricing pressure reflects a softer, polarized economy. Emphasis shifted from volume to efficiency.

Drivers of growth & planning for next year

[00:02:08–00:03:55]

Brands that entered Q4 strong on new customer acquisition outperformed. New customer growth sets the ceiling for the following year. Planning should focus on identifying past revenue peaks and troughs, understanding what created them, and restructuring the marketing calendar to raise the floor and create new peaks.

Practical planning exercise

[00:03:00–00:04:10]

Recommendation to visualize weekly revenue data, identify peaks and troughs, and layer marketing initiatives on top to uncover opportunities for incremental gains when prior launches or moments are unavailable.

LLMs, SEO/GEO, and “answer engine optimization” skepticism

[00:04:20–00:08:55]

Basic hygiene matters (clean product feeds into LLMs via Shopify or tools like catalog integrations), but there is no proven, repeatable way to “game” LLM optimization today. Most agencies sell fear-of-missing-out rather than clear ROI. Long-term value comes from being a primary source of authoritative content across the web.

Content strategy in an LLM-driven world

[00:09:00–00:10:20]

LLM visibility is a byproduct of publishing high-quality, authoritative content over long periods. Being the most trusted source on core questions matters more than short-term tactics.

2026 eCommerce outlook & major structural shifts

[00:10:45–00:18:50]

Key themes include disproportionate winners shaping the market, TikTok Shop and affiliate-driven growth models, declining cost of creative, and disintermediation away from traditional websites. Commerce increasingly happens in feeds, marketplaces, LLMs, and emerging interfaces rather than owned sites alone.

TikTok Shops & affiliate-driven growth model

[00:11:30–00:15:10]

Example of rapid growth driven by massive affiliate networks that shift creative production risk away from brands. This flips traditional marketing cost structures but may be fragile as incentives mature and competition increases.

Social commerce, live shopping, and content everywhere

[00:15:10–00:16:25]

Affiliate enablement—not just in-feed checkout—made social commerce viable. Content creation expands across the entire organization, from manufacturing to fulfillment, as everything becomes source material for marketing.

Global commerce & infrastructure gaps

[00:16:30–00:18:50]

Growth increasingly comes from international expansion, aided by improved payments and stablecoins. Product, inventory, and cost data infrastructure remains fragmented and underdeveloped, creating operational risk and opportunity.

Affiliate saturation & market timing risks

[00:19:00–00:21:15]

By the time a tactic is widely discussed, the easy arbitrage is usually gone. Affiliate and influencer markets mature quickly, driving up costs and compressing margins, similar to past cycles like Facebook ads or NFTs.

AI-generated creative: what actually works

[00:21:25–00:25:25]

Research suggests AI performs worse when labeled as AI or constrained by strict brand rules, but can outperform humans when unconstrained. The core advantage is removing human creative bias and enabling extreme variation at scale.

Brand risk vs. creative exploration

[00:24:05–00:25:25]

AI excels at doing things brands would never attempt. The value lies in experimentation and exploration, not perfection or safety-first design.

AI personas, avatars, and scale strategies

[00:25:45–00:27:15]

The asset is no longer the account or follower base; distribution is algorithmic. Thousands of AI-driven accounts or personas can test content simultaneously, letting performance determine winners.

Creator loyalty & relationship economics

[00:28:45–00:32:45]

Creators behave transactionally unless genuine relational equity exists. Paying the most money works, but so does investing time, support, and mentorship early—before creators generate value.

Long-term mindset vs. hype cycles

[00:33:00–00:35:45]

Advocacy for a 10-year horizon: focus on doing a few things well, layering in new capabilities gradually. Hyper-growth stories are distractions. Invest in future bets only once core channels are healthy.

Paid media outlook: Meta, Google, YouTube, and beyond

[00:35:50–00:40:25]

Search remains critical and highly category-dependent. YouTube can be powerful for brands with multi-channel distribution, but requires incrementality-based measurement. Asset-level strategy (short-form vertical video) should drive distribution across Meta, Google/YouTube, TikTok, and AppLovin.

Creative trends & experimentation with AI

[00:40:45–00:46:30]

Effective AI use favors novel, impossible, or playful concepts (animals, exaggerated visuals, unexpected perspectives) over hyper-realistic humans. The opportunity lies in producing massive creative volume cheaply and testing aggressively.
