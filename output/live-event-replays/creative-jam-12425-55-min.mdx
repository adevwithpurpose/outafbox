Creative Jam 12/4/25 (55 min)

Live Event Replays

CTC Creative Strategist Ben Liebman answers members' creative questions and helps ideate new creative directions.

Timestamps:

1. Holiday Gifting Strategy for Dance Masterclass (00:00:00–00:13:30)

Nicholas asks about gifting-related offer structures and holiday creative direction.

Simple, straightforward discounts outperform complicated “buy this, get that” mechanics.

Dance Masterclass is a niche gift; messaging must stay extremely clear.

Split-screen ads and workout-related static creatives continue to perform well.

Current ads are missing clear direction about

what

is being gifted: gift of dance, gift of movement, gift of mastery, etc.

“Perfect gift for…” overlays work better than pure Christmas-themed branding.

2. Evergreen vs Seasonal Creative & Gifting Positioning (00:03:29–00:09:31)

Discussion on framing DM for holiday gifting and ongoing use.

Evergreen UGC content continues to outperform heavily branded holiday ads.

A simple, consistent overlay like “The perfect gift for dancers” is more effective than heavy holiday framing.

Alternative wording unlocks new audiences (e.g., “perfect gift for someone who wants to work out and enjoy it”).

Use reviews or UGC clips of people doing the workouts with gift messaging layered on top.

Digital products shine during last-minute gifting windows (post-shipping deadlines).

3. Timing of Christmas Ads (00:47:16–00:49:50)

It’s not too early to run Christmas ads — you could start as early as October.

Expect a strong spike once shipping deadlines pass and digital gifts become more appealing.

4. Examples of “Winning” Gifting Brands (00:46:42–00:48:20)

Some categories naturally excel at gifting (apparel, furniture, luxury goods).

Dance Masterclass must lean on emotional or aspirational messaging, not category conventions.

5. December Discount Strategy Post-BFCM (00:13:30–00:16:30)

Raz asks about full-month December discounts.

Discount strategy depends on BFCM:

If acquisition was strong, you can ease off.

If not, December is still a prime buying window.

A flat 15% off all month is a simple and effective option.

Watch for customer perceptions if BFCM discounts were deeper.

6. Creative Differentiation, Audience Strategy & the “Godzilla” Concept (00:21:14–00:35:55)

Ben explains creative fatigue and Meta’s algorithmic behavior.

Meta's algorithm favors top performers (“Godzillas”) and resists reallocating spend.

New ads must be meaningfully different in message, idea, and visual direction to break through.

Sometimes new creatives should be tested in separate ad sets so they don't get overshadowed.

Old top performers from previous years can be revived, but must be updated enough to qualify as “new.”

Use calendar moments beyond Christmas (New Year, Valentine’s Day) with contextual framing.

7. Using AI-Generated Creators in Ads (00:16:30–00:23:15)

Raz explores whether AI creators can scale message delivery.

AI creator strategy

can

work, but must first be validated by results.

Useful for scaling creative diversity (many faces delivering proven scripts).

Should be tested separately from “Godzilla” ads to avoid being overshadowed.
