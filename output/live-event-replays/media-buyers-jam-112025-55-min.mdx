Media Buyers' Jam 11/20/25 (55 min)

Live Event Replays

CTC Director of Paid Social Delaney Quinn answers members questions about Black Friday prep, including:

Pre-Black-Friday Technical Checklist

Friday & Monday Peak Timing

Cyber Monday Offer Changes & Creative Refresh Strategy

And more.

Timestamped Summary:

00:00–01:45 — Creative Aspect Ratios & Ad Production Efficiency

Discussion opens on whether to create ads in multiple aspect ratios or rely on Meta to auto-adjust.

Recommended formats:

1:1 or 4:5 for feed

,

9:16 for Stories

.

Importance of avoiding blank space/weird stretching.

Creative efficiency clarified: most brands build

two

formats, sometimes three.

01:45–06:35 — Cost Caps, Efficiency, and Early Black Friday Behavior

David asks about a cost-cap campaign overshooting target CPA leading up to Black Friday.

Delaney explains:

Consumers often

wait for the best sale

, depressing conversion rate.

Adding promo creatives to evergreen campaigns resets learnings.

Best practice:

separate evergreen vs. promo campaigns

.

Use funnel metrics—CTR, conversion rate, view-content → ATC ratio—to determine if users are waiting or if audience quality is low.

Recommendation:

pull back slightly

, adjust cost caps, and break promos into their own campaigns.

06:35–14:05 — Identifying Quality Traffic & High-AOV Buyer Behavior

Starlet asks about evaluating intent for high-ticket products.

Key insights:

The past ~30 days show general

consumer softness

, especially on high-AOV items.

Check CPM, CPC, CTR trends: higher CPM + lower conversion suggests

message mismatch

.

Ensure promo creative also explains the product, not just the discount.

Use

video-view audiences

or site-traffic buckets to retarget with promo reminders.

Evergreen often gets attribution because high-AOV customers need

multiple touchpoints

.

14:05–20:30 — Tactical Black Friday Media Buying (Day-Of Playbook)

Nicholas asks what media buyers

actually do

during BFCM weekend.

Delaney outlines the real-time playbook:

Scale budgets Thursday night

(not Friday morning) to catch early-morning demand surges.

Expect peak demand around

8 AM MST on Friday

.

On Black Friday:

Actively

push/pull budgets

, turn campaigns on/off throughout the day (unlike normal days).

Monitor Shopify revenue trends to correlate spend.

Launch multiple versions:

Cost-cap

High-ROAS

Highest-volume

Evergreen-with-promo

Different click windows (1-day vs 7-day)

Cyber Monday:

Peak later in the day (~7 PM MST)

Don’t cut too early.

20:30–23:40 — Cyber Monday Offer Changes & Creative Refresh Strategy

Matt asks whether to change offers for Cyber Monday.

Delaney strongly advises

not changing offers

if possible:

Causes campaign resets on the biggest day of the year.

Introduce new Cyber Monday creatives in

new campaigns

, not inside existing high-performing ones.

Maintain creative momentum:

Refresh during the promo period (Fri, Sun/Tue) so users don’t see the same ad repeatedly.

23:40–29:30 — When to Launch Sales & Running Promos for Cold vs Warm Audiences

Early launch can pull some sales forward, but:

Black Friday still captures the largest share

.

Launching earlier in the week is fine—doesn’t necessarily create less-qualified buyers.

For promos to cold vs warm audiences:

Same creative often works for both.

Warm audiences can get slightly tweaked “come back” language.

The

discount is the strategy

.

29:30–32:25 — Lead Gen Strategy for BFCM

Nicholas asks about early-access lead gen.

Delaney:

Don’t judge lead-gen by ROAS; focus on

CPL

.

Early-access messaging usually produces

much cheaper leads

vs evergreen.

Track backend quality through email + SMS performance.

32:25–36:40 — Ad Account Shutdowns, Daily Spend Limits & New Pixels

A user describes a hacked account and issues with new accounts getting shut down at higher daily spend.

Delaney:

New accounts often have

daily spend limits

; Meta support can confirm the cap.

New pixels produce

cheap CPMs

because they lack data and find low-quality audiences first.

Recommend establishing backup social channels (TikTok, Snapchat) if stability is uncertain.

36:40–39:10 — Pre-Black-Friday Technical Checklist

Core operational checklist:

Check:

Billing (backup credit cards, credit line)

Pixel health

Catalog integrity

EMQ scores

Build

backup campaigns

ahead of time to avoid approval delays during the peak weekend.

39:10–43:20 — Friday & Monday Peak Timing and How to Adjust Budgets

Black Friday:

Peak is early morning.

Increase budgets Thursday night.

Spend aggressively in the morning.

Pull back Friday late afternoon/evening.

Cyber Monday:

Peak is in the evening.

Don’t cut spend early.

Increase Sunday night.

Drastically reduce Monday night for Tuesday.

43:20–49:00 — Budgeting Cadence & Adjustments Throughout the Weekend

Budgets are adjusted:

Thurs night → up

Fri afternoon → moderate pullback

Sun night → up

Mon night →

hard

pullback

Unlike normal days,

you

should

make multiple changes

during BFCM.

49:00–End — Cost Caps & Scaling

Cost caps should be adjusted

lightly

, not loosened dramatically.

Efficiency generally improves during Black Friday, so caps don't need major widening unless pacing is slow.
