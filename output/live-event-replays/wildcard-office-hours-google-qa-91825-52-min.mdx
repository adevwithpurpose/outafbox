Wildcard Office Hours: Google Q&A 9/18/25 (52 min)

Live Event Replays

In this Q&A, CTC Director of Paid Search Rachel Kwon answers members' questions on Pmax best practices, managing branded vs non-branded, advertising a niche product, and more.

Timestamps:

[00:00:00 – 00:06:45] Launching Google Campaigns with Low Search Volume

Discussion on how to approach Google when search demand is low.

Strategies: start with Performance Max (PMax) using NCA bidding, add non-brand search with brand exclusions, and run brand lift or search lift studies.

Caution against “feed-only” PMax (removing assets) except in rare cases. Image testing on PLAs (shopping ads) only matters if conversion rates are broadly weak.

[00:07:05 – 00:12:10] Best Practices for PMax and Branded Terms

Debate on whether to exclude branded traffic from PMax.

Suggested structure:

Run Standard Shopping for branded.

Use PMax for non-branded prospecting.

Alternatively, run two PMax campaigns (one brand-included, one brand-excluded).

Importance of measuring incrementality with search lift, brand lift, or conversion lift.

PMax tends to over-index on remarketing without exclusions or NCA bidding.

[00:12:28 – 00:16:03] Expanding Beyond Meta Into Google

Question from an agency testing Meta ads, wondering if they should master Meta before learning Google.

Advice: No need to be an expert in one platform first. Each platform has different strengths, demographics, and buying cycles. Better to diversify earlier and learn in parallel.

[00:16:10 – 00:20:45] Key Developments in Google Ads

Biggest changes include:

“Power Pack”: combining AI Max, Search, Demand Gen, and PMax.

Improved customer match and new bidding types (acquisition-only, retention, lapsed customers).

Demand Gen + video/CTV ads.

Channel reporting for PMax (visibility but not yet actionable).

Google now allows more audience-level bidding, similar to Meta.

[00:20:50 – 00:26:36] Entering Google Ads with a High-Value Brand

A men’s luxury apparel brand asks how to start on Google with limited bandwidth.

Recommendations:

Start with branded search to defend position and drive high ROAS.

Run branded search alongside branded PMax.

Add Standard Shopping and eventually expand into hero products/non-brand search.

Emphasis on diversification to avoid relying only on PMax.

[00:27:00 – 00:34:13] Local Lead Gen and the Future of Search vs. ChatGPT

A construction/remodeling business case: how to use Search + PMax together.

Recommendation: run non-brand search and non-brand PMax.

Use geo-targeting.

Consider Demand Gen if video creative is available.

Broader discussion: ChatGPT vs Google. Ads on ChatGPT are piloting but Google is not going away soon. Both can be used together for different behaviors.

[00:34:30 – 00:41:27] Strategy for a Niche Product (Hunting Suit)

Niche products risk wasted spend with broad PMax.

Recommended campaign mix:

60% shopping (PMax or Standard).

10–15% branded search.

Remainder in Demand Gen/non-brand search.

Diversification prevents over-reliance on PMax and protects against issues like Merchant Center disapprovals.

[00:41:40 – 00:51:32] Scaling Supplements Brand in Italy

Brand profitably scaling Meta (30K/month) but struggled with Google (3–5K/month unprofitable).

Possible cause: poor execution/agency setup, not the platform.

Google can work for supplements in Italy; rollout must be gradual with strong keyword research and targeted bidding.

Recommended next steps:

Start with branded/non-brand search.

Use Demand Gen with video assets (repurpose Meta creatives).

Leverage customer match lists and CRM data.

Don’t exclude returning customers—brand has strong LTV.

For management: if Google isn’t top priority, consider a freelancer or light consultancy over a full agency.
