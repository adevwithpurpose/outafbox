Media Buyers Jam 1/29/26 (53 min)

Live Event Replays

Watch as CTC Director of Paid Social Delaney Quinn answers members' questions on scaling an account where nothing's working, how to approach creative testing, optimizing catalog ads, and more.

Timestamps:

I. Troubleshooting Low AOV and Scaling Challenges

[00:00:00] - Account Overhaul:

A buyer describes the frustration of testing hundreds of campaigns, including ROAS campaigns, flex ads, and cost caps, without finding a stable way to scale a low Average Order Value (AOV) brand.

[00:02:10] - Returning to Basics:

The advisor suggests looking at historical winners, specifically an old "Advantage+" campaign that achieved a 1.78 ROAS, and emphasizes the importance of single-item focus versus complex bundles.

[00:04:45] - The Cost of Resetting:

A warning is given against constantly launching new campaigns. Every time a campaign is reset, the algorithm's learnings are lost. The recommendation is to relaunch successful past ads to build momentum and account "backbone."

II. Creative Testing and Account Structure

[00:10:40] - The Flaw in "Test vs. Scale" Structures:

Delaney advises against having separate "test" and "scale" campaigns. Moving a winning creative from a test campaign to a scale campaign often fails because Meta recognizes the "Entity ID" and may deprioritize the duplicate in the auction.

[00:13:20] - Consolidation is Key:

For smaller budgets (e.g., $5k/month), it is better to consolidate creatives into a single ad set. This allows the algorithm to reach the "active" phase faster rather than getting stuck in the "learning" phase across multiple segmented ad sets.

[00:15:55] - Trusting the Algorithm:

Media buyers must accept that the algorithm will choose which creatives to spend on. Forcing spend on a creative that the algorithm has deprioritized leads to "wishy-washy" performance.

III. Market Trends and Tracking Issues

[00:17:50] - Recent Performance Dips:

Several brands reported a "softness" in performance during late January. The team notes this may be a seasonal shift in consumer behavior or a technical tracking issue.

[00:18:40] - Shopify Pixel Update:

A critical update on January 13th changed Shopify pixels from "always on" to an "optimized" state. This change can potentially impact conversion tracking data for pixels that don't have high data volumes.

IV. Dynamic Ads and Catalog Management

[00:19:35] - Optimizing DABA:

For Dynamic Ads for Broad Audiences (DABA), it is recommended to narrow the catalog to "Best Sellers" and monitor if the product mix has shifted, as Meta may suddenly prioritize products that don't resonate as well.

[00:20:10] - Campaign Separation:

Always break DABA (catalog) ads into their own campaigns. If mixed with statics or videos, the DABA ads will often "cannibalize" 80–90% of the spend, preventing other creatives from getting enough data to learn.

V. UGC and Creative Diversity

[00:23:20] - Health and Wellness Strategy:

In crowded markets like supplements, User-Generated Content (UGC) that shows a "day in the life" or how the product works is essential to break through the noise.

[00:32:00] - The Entity ID and Similarity:

Meta uses "Entity ID" to label creatives. If 150 ads look too similar (e.g., just changing a headline or an end card), the algorithm will treat them as the same and deprioritize them. True creative diversity—different rooms, lighting, and personas—is required for scaling.

VI. Bidding, Budgets, and the Learning Phase

[00:27:00] - Cost Controls:

Using a "Cost Cap" or "Min ROAS" with an inflated budget allows Meta to spend more on high-efficiency days (like Saturdays) and pull back on low-efficiency days, reducing the need for manual budget adjustments.

[00:31:55] - Don't Delete Ads:

Deleting ads is discouraged as it makes historical reporting difficult. Instead, focus on a structure where you don't need to move ads between sets.

[00:48:40] - Exiting the Learning Phase:

To exit "Learning," an ad set needs 50 conversions in a 7-day period. If a brand is struggling to hit this, advisors suggest opening up the attribution window to "7-day click / 1-day view" to feed more signals into the account.
