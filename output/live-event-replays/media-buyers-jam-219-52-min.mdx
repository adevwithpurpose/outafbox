Media Buyers Jam 2/19 (52 min)

Live Event Replays

Watch as CTC Director of Paid Social Delaney Quinn answers members questions on everything Meta.

Timestamps:

Account Performance & Market Trends

[00:00:00] Ad Account Softness:

A discussion on the "post-holiday slump." While some softness is expected after Valentine’s Day and President’s Day, a consistent 8-day decline might suggest internal account issues rather than just market trends.

[00:01:35] Holiday Marketing Moments:

Recommendation to lean into holidays even without a specific discount. Aligning products with "top of mind" moments (like gifting) helps maintain resonance with shoppers.

[00:08:43] Dealing with Volatility:

After consolidating campaigns, day-to-day performance swings are normal. Meta optimizes on a 7-day window; avoid "gut reaction" changes based on a single bad day.

Campaign Strategy & Optimization

[00:02:40] Fixing Spend Issues:

If a ROAS-target campaign isn’t spending, try "Highest Volume" (lowest cost) to get data moving, or switch to Value Optimization.

[00:05:12] Value Optimization for AOV:

For accounts struggling with low Average Order Value (AOV), Value Optimization can help find higher-spending customers without the rigid delivery constraints of a Minimum ROAS target.

[00:16:43] Managing Ad Limits:

If you are hitting Meta’s 250-ad limit, pause older ads that have spent less than $5 in the last week. Consolidating winners is more effective than keeping "zombie" ads running.

[00:24:50] Cost Caps & Learning:

Lowering a cost cap by 5% is safe; however, a 20% shift will likely reset the algorithm and send the campaign back into the "Learning Phase."

[00:27:05] The Consolidation Debate:

When moving to a consolidated setup, a formal AB test may "muddy the waters" if you're testing a new campaign against an established one. Sometimes, a clean manual migration is better.

Creative Strategy & AI

[00:11:45] Creative Diversification:

To beat a winning ad, don't just tweak headlines. Try new formats (UGC), different settings, or entirely new value props to reach different segments of the algorithm.

[00:19:35] Refreshing Founder Ads:

If a high-performing video starts to fatigue, try changing the thumbnail first or reshooting the successful hook in a new setting.

[00:22:15] The Reality of AI Ads:

Current consensus is that AI video tools (like Higgsfield) are great for volume but often fail to outperform real UGC. "Humanesque" AI can still feel "creepy" to certain demographics and often gets brand details (like logos) wrong.

[00:50:50] "State of the Union" Best Performer:

Currently,

UGC Statics

(still images pulled from UGC videos with a simple headline overlay) are driving high efficiency across most accounts.

Technical Tips & Hacks

[00:14:50] Managing AI Enhancements:

Meta often resets "Advantage+ Creative" enhancements after publishing. Use the mass-edit dropdown on the ad level to ensure all AI enhancements are turned off post-launch.

[00:26:20] Flexible Ads vs. Aesthetics:

Flexible ads prioritize performance over looks. They may place a square image in a vertical Story slot if the algorithm thinks it will convert, regardless of how it looks visually.

[00:41:05] TikTok Strategy:

For TikTok, "Smart Plus" campaigns are showing promise. Because it is a scroll-heavy platform, including "View-Through" attribution is often necessary to get enough signal for the algorithm.

[00:46:05] Catalog Ads (DABA):

If Advantage+ Catalog ads are underperforming, check your "match rate" in Commerce Manager and ensure you aren't pulling in low-margin or out-of-stock products.

Full Funnel & Awareness

[00:39:45] Awareness Campaigns:

Allocating 5–10% of the budget to Reach/Awareness can help "warm up" audiences.

[00:47:15] Traffic vs. Video Views:

For top-of-funnel, "Landing Page Views" are often superior to "Video Views" because they allow you to cookie the user for more accurate retargeting later.
