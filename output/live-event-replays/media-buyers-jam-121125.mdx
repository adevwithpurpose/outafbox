Media Buyers' Jam 12/11/25

Live Event Replays

CTC Director of Paid Media Delaney Quinn answers members' questions on media buying and BFCM outcomes.

Timestamps:

Post-BFCM Performance & Observations

[00:00:23] Recovery and 2026 Planning

: Discussion on the immediate shift from BFCM recaps to preparing for 2026

.

[00:00:38] Comparative Success

: This BFCM was noted as more successful and less chaotic compared to previous years, despite economic shifts in buying power

.

[00:01:38] Offer Impact

: Simple, flat offers (e.g., 20% off sitewide) performed better than complex, "fancy" stacked discounts, which tended to confuse consumers

.

[00:02:10] Black Friday vs. Cyber Monday

: Performance varied by brand, but many saw Black Friday as the primary takeoff point, while others saw Cyber Monday shoot up in costs and CPMs due to increased competition

.

Pricing Strategies & Testing

[00:04:29] High Discount Scaling

: A case study where a 50%+ discount allowed a brand to scale its budget 8–9x daily averages

.

[00:05:01] Price Elasticity

: Discussion on testing lower price points via Meta A/B tests to see if increased demand offsets lower margins

.

[00:05:47] Current Trends

: Most brands are currently raising prices due to increased fees and using "buy now before prices go up" messaging

.

Holiday Gifting & Digital Products

[00:07:43] Shipping Cutoffs

: Many brands use mid-December shipping cutoffs to push last-minute gifting demand

.

[00:08:48] Digital Product Strategy

: For digital goods that don't require shipping, the peak demand often occurs on December 24th and 25th, when physical goods are no longer an option for shoppers

.

Health, Wellness & "Q5" Strategy

[00:10:41] Manufacturing Urgency

: Strategies for non-gifting brands (like supplements) to maintain momentum in December using free gifts or minor discounts

.

[00:11:00] New Year Preparation

: Health brands are advised to prepare "New Year, New You" creative to launch on Christmas Day (Q5), taking advantage of cheaper auction costs as big-box retailers pull out

.

[00:12:35] Spend Curve

: Anticipating high demand in early January with a natural taper toward the end of the month

.

Meta Ad Account Technicals

[00:13:17] Cost Controls vs. Highest Volume

: Recommendations to test Minimum ROAS, Cost Caps, and Highest Volume simultaneously during peak seasons to see which resonates with the specific brand

.

[00:14:48] Highest Volume Success

: Notably, "Highest Volume" bidding was more efficient this year than in the past, often hitting targets without the usual runaway spend

.

[00:16:38] Awareness & Traffic

: A critique of "Video View" optimizations for being "unqualified";

Delaney recommends "Landing Page Views" or "Add to Cart" for more meaningful awareness traffic

.

[00:17:44] Value Rules

: Using Meta’s value rules to bid up or down on specific demographics (like age) rather than using hard exclusions to keep CPMs lower

.

Apparel & DPA Strategies

[00:20:01] High-Ticket Apparel

: Men's apparel brands selling items from $175 to $800 found Cost Caps more effective for scaling than Minimum ROAS

.

[00:20:53] Dynamic Product Ads (DPA)

: Discussion on maintaining a "clean feed" and testing segmented product sets (e.g., blazers vs. shirts) versus a general all-catalog DPA

.

[00:23:02] Ad-Level Setup

: A tip to set up product catalogs at the

ad level

rather than the campaign level to allow for better consolidation and algorithmic decision-making

.

Privacy & Tracking Challenges

[00:39:40] Marketing to the "Unmarketable"

: Handling a customer base where 70% use VPNs and script blockers

.

00:40:48] Broad Targeting

: For privacy-heavy audiences, Delaney suggests going "totally broad" and relying on messaging rather than pixel-based targeting or exclusions

.

[00:43:39] Lookalikes

: While often considered outdated, 10% lookalike audiences may still provide a useful signal for brands with difficult-to-track users

.

Andromeda & Creative Intelligence

[00:47:43] Algorithmic Evolution

: Discussion on how the "Andromeda" algorithm now "reads" creative content

.

[00:48:08] Promo vs. Evergreen

: This year, promo-specific ads performed nearly as well as evergreen ads because the algorithm could identify relevant "Black Friday" text within the image/video and serve it to qualified users

.
