Tactics with Taylor Live 10/30/25

Live Event Replays

CTC CEO Taylor Holiday answers members' questions about high AOV strategy, BFCM offer construction, how to give team members the right KPIs, and more.

Timestamps:

[00:00:00–00:07:35] Premium Brand with 4K AOV Facing Slow Sales

Topic:

Diagnosing stagnation and Q4 strategy

Taylor advises evaluating

price elasticity

—price hikes from tariffs may have reduced demand.

Paid media inefficiency:

rising ad costs and seasonal slowness make Meta less viable for such high AOV.

Recommends

direct outreach/sales

to ultra-wealthy customers rather than scaling paid ads.

Suggests small

remarketing campaigns

only, focusing on

concierge-style selling

and

human-led outreach

.

[00:08:00–00:20:15] Attribution Models and ROAS Interpretation (Tammy)

Topic:

Reconciling Meta and Google Analytics data

Taylor explains differences between

7-day click/1-day view

vs

last-click attribution

.

Meta’s 7-day click usually best approximates true incremental value.

Walkthrough of comparing

Meta ROAS to first-time customer revenue

to assess alignment.

Advises ignoring outlier orders skewing data and focusing on overall correlation trends.

Discusses

budget scaling

and avoiding overreacting to short-term ROAS drops due to low order volume.

Clarifies that

2.6 ROAS is strong performance

; scaling should depend on break-even threshold (~3.3 target).

[00:20:20–00:28:15] Structuring Paid Media Team Incentives (Jacqueline)

Topic:

Setting KPIs for media buyer and creative strategist

Recommends

avoiding ratio metrics (ROAS/MER)

for individual incentives; they discourage scaling.

Focus instead on

contribution profit

and

new customer volume growth

at or above break-even efficiency.

Both roles share a unified goal:

grow ad account volume profitably

.

Creative strategist shouldn’t have separate proxy KPIs (CTR, CPM)—shared accountability is key.

Incentive structure: clear cost-to-value relationship and profit-sharing

after covering organizational cost

.

[00:28:25–00:32:00] CPM Increases and Meta Platform Costs (David)

Topic:

Reacting to Meta’s rising ad prices

Taylor frames advertisers as

“market takers”

—you can’t control auction costs.

Meta’s leverage remains strong due to lack of effective alternatives.

For brands with low LTV (e.g., wedding rings), rising CPMs pose structural challenges.

Advises only reallocating spend if a demonstrably better demand source exists—otherwise,

Meta remains default best option

.

[00:32:05–00:36:00] Setting In-Platform Targets Without Statlas (Josh)

Topic:

Calculating meta ROAS targets

Base Meta 7-day click ROAS target on desired

A/MER

(account-level MER).

May adjust by

~1.2x factor

to reflect average incrementality variance.

If business ignores OPEX in eCom-only P&L, ensure target still creates contribution profit.

Suggests

Meta target ≈ 1.5–1.7

range, depending on aggressiveness and operating margin.

[00:36:05–00:41:55] Subscription Offer Testing: 1, 3, and 6-Month Plans (Nathan)

Topic:

Evaluating CAC/LTV across subscription options

AOV increased with multi-month bundles, but unclear long-term retention impact.

Taylor recommends waiting for cohort maturity (3–6 months) before conclusions.

Weighs

cash flow vs. churn risk

—prefer testing both concurrently through Black Friday.

Shorter-term subscriptions provide better feedback loops and lower CAC risk.

Suggests using Black Friday to test both 1-month and 3/6-month plans for real data.

[00:42:00–00:43:55] Lower Efficiency Before Black Friday (Josh)

Topic:

Adjusting ROAS targets in Q4 ramp-up

Pre–BFCM period shows higher

delayed attribution

, so slightly lower short-term ROAS acceptable.

During BFCM week, efficiency expectations rise as attribution tightens.

Suggests

15% temporary reduction in ROAS target

during early November ramp-up.

[00:43:55–00:47:35] Black Friday / Cyber Monday Planning

Topic:

Offer design, pacing, and flexibility

Prepare backup offers for weak early performance.

Expect revenue peak early on Black Friday (8–11 AM PST) and late on Cyber Monday.

Use

hourly spend pacing

and

pre-approved high-volume campaigns

for backup.

Plan “worst-case” contingencies for ad delivery issues or soft sales.

[00:47:35–00:49:00+] Offer Design and Timing Strategy

Topic:

BFCM offer structure and timing

Peak conversion still occurs on Black Friday/Cyber Monday; early-November deals only help slightly.

Clear, simple

sitewide percentage discounts

outperform complex bundles.

Layer

flash sales

on overstock SKUs to sustain engagement.

On Cyber Monday, vary structure (e.g., BOGO or tiered discounts) to maintain novelty.

Recommends exploring

Cofounder’s “Mission” Offer Database

for examples and trend data.

Final takeaway: Stay adaptable, prioritize clarity, and prep contingencies for ad pacing.
