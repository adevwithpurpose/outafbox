Media Buyers' Jam 10/9/25 (53 min)

Live Event Replays

CTC Director of Paid Media Delaney Quinn answers members' questions on:

Bid types and when to use them,

Scaling DABA campaigns,

When to launch your holiday offers

Why you should inflate budgets

And more.

Timestamps:

[00:00:00–00:04:25] Cost Caps vs. Highest Value Bidding

Discussion on campaign consolidation and bidding strategy (cost cap, highest value, highest volume).

Delaney advises mixing highest volume and value bidding to balance reach and efficiency.

Creative strategy: use value bidding during promos/launches, not evergreen periods.

Apparel account consolidation tips: organize by product line (tops, bottoms) and monitor inventory to avoid fragmentation.

For acquisition campaigns, exclude all existing customers (pixel, CRM, Shopify) to prevent retargeting overlap.

[00:04:30–00:06:10] Flexible Media Setup in Meta Ads

David asks about using Meta’s “flexible setup” for new creative launches.

Delaney says most CTC accounts stick with single static/video assets due to cropping issues with Meta’s AI resizing.

Flexible formats expand placement options but require close monitoring for formatting errors.

Multi-advertiser placement toggle is generally safe to use.

[00:06:15–00:12:50] Scaling DABA Campaigns and Creative Mix

Matt’s high-end apparel brand (custom dress shirts) struggles to scale weekday spend.

Delaney notes weekday softness is normal; analyze performance by day of week.

DABAs perform well for apparel but often overspend on existing customers.

Recommendation: combine DABA (catalog ads) with ASC creative for brand storytelling and broader reach.

Separate creative campaigns from catalog ads to avoid cannibalization.

Launching new high-AOV lines (e.g., suits/blazers) should use separate campaigns or ad sets with A/B budgets, not CBO.

Use both Cost Cap and MinROAS strategies simultaneously, depending on product type and promo timing.

[00:12:55–00:21:20] Gender Targeting and Creative Pipeline Planning

Nicholas discusses gender-heavy targeting (90% female purchasers).

Delaney advises leaving targeting open—Meta self-optimizes—but it’s fine to skew toward females if spend doesn’t match purchase ratio.

During holidays, keep male targeting on at low scale for gift traffic; turn off post-holiday if inefficient.

Creative pipeline planning: CTC uses a “creative demand model” to estimate needed volume (e.g., 200 new ads/month).

Blend evergreen (60–70%) and promo creative, mapped to personas and awareness stages.

Delaney emphasizes evergreen creative still performs strongly in holidays.

Awareness-level creative is rare in CTC accounts focused on conversion, but important for brand storytelling.

[00:21:25–00:25:55] Holiday Campaign Timing & Strategy

Tammy asks about why some brands spend more in November than December.

Delaney: most eComm brands peak in November due to Black Friday buildup; December sales tend to be lighter.

Seasonal exceptions (e.g., food/meat brands) should lean into Thanksgiving rather than gift-driven messaging.

Meta trends: holiday ads are launching earlier each year (even in October).

Caution about “pull-forward effect”—early launches can move revenue earlier but shouldn’t cannibalize Black Friday.

Refresh creative regularly to maintain engagement over longer holiday periods.

[00:26:00–00:33:40] Structuring Holiday Campaigns

Cody and Matt ask how to structure campaigns around holidays.

Use separate campaigns for evergreen, holiday, and promo messages.

Keep cost controls consistent—don’t loosen them.

For Black Friday:

Launch dedicated campaign (not ad set).

Use “highest volume” backup campaign to ensure spending starts on time.

Avoid adjusting bids frequently—confuses learning.

After initial ramp-up, turn off high-volume campaigns to protect efficiency.

For sequential promos (early Nov + Black Friday): use distinct campaigns and refreshed creative even if offers are similar.

Combine Cost Cap, MinROAS, and Highest Volume campaigns for maximum reach and stability.

[00:34:00–00:40:27] Bid Strategy & Creative Rotation for Beauty Brand

Rod discusses improving ROAS on a beauty account.

His highest volume campaigns drove spend but hurt efficiency; cost caps performed better but limited spend.

Delaney: move all strong creatives into cost cap campaigns; Meta rewards newness.

If hitting 85% daily budget, increase it.

One creative dominating performance is fine—refresh regularly before fatigue.

Split bidding:

Use MinROAS for high-AOV products.

Use Cost Cap for core/lower-AOV SKUs.

Avoid duplicate creatives across multiple campaigns to reduce internal competition.

Keep bid caps live even if under-spending—they can perform well during peak efficiency days.

[00:40:35–00:43:25] Inflated Budgets & Spend Control

Nicholas asks about “inflated budgets.”

Delaney: use inflated budgets across all bid types to allow Meta flexibility when efficiency is high.

For promos, inflate more heavily (up to 3x); for evergreen, smaller padding (10–25%).

Even if daily performance fluctuates, results even out over seven-day cycles.

Bid caps may underspend but can excel during high-efficiency moments (holidays).

[00:43:30–00:47:20] ASC Campaign Optimization & Cost Cap Calibration

David describes ASC campaign underspending despite high efficiency.

Delaney: if ROAS is well above goal, no need to loosen cost caps.

Cost cap should align with AOV ÷ ROAS target; if current CPA is below target, maintain settings.

Avoid frequent bid tweaks—Meta self-adjusts for volatility.

[00:46:20–End] Meta Updates & Holiday Focus

Meta’s current emphasis is on holiday readiness: value suite, incremental attribution, AI-generated creative.

No major product releases—Q4 focus is campaign performance, attribution updates, and creative automation.
