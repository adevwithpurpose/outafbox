Growth Strategy w/Brian Sakansky 1/16/26 (51 min)

Live Event Replays

Senior Growth Strategist Brian Sakansky answers members' questions on ideal team structure, how to approach high-AOV accounts, building a marketing calendar, and more.

Timestamps:

Incremental Attribution & Meta Strategy

[00:00:30] - Results of 7-Day Incremental Attribution:

Brian notes that testing this setting has yielded variable results; while it can improve platform performance and channel incrementality for some, it falls flat for others

.

[00:01:04] - Scenarios for Testing:

He recommends testing this when there is a high percentage of "engaged" vs. "new" audiences or when reported platform ROAS is high but MER (Marketing Efficiency Ratio) is not making sense.

[00:03:30] - Managing Long Consideration Cycles:

For businesses with long purchase cycles (e.g., three weeks), Brian advises setting looser targets initially to help Meta exit the "learning phase" before tightening constraints

.

Team Structure & Specialized Roles

[00:10:55] - Hiring Evolution:

Teams typically start with one consolidated person (Growth Marketing Manager or Paid Media Manager)

.

As spending increases, roles become specialized into Paid Social (Meta, TikTok) and Paid Search (Google, Bing).

[00:13:57] - Expertise Requirements:

Brian emphasizes that it is difficult to find a single in-house person who can manage every channel excellently, as each requires significant expertise

.

[00:18:03] - Compensation Expectations:

A senior growth leader overseeing multiple channels and strategy typically costs between $150,000 and $250,000 per year.

Creative Philosophy & Production

[00:23:10] - Optimizing Catalog (DABA) Ads:

Effective dynamic ads require a highly "dialed" feed with detailed titles, descriptions, and custom tags rather than out-of-the-box solutions.

[00:26:15] - Winner Concentration:

Rather than fixating on a 4% success rate, teams should perform an 80/20 analysis to see which ads are spending above a certain threshold and meeting ROAS targets

.

[00:41:15] - AI and Scaling Output:

Brian encourages the use of generative AI tools (mentioning "Nano Banana") to increase creative volume, as the modern algorithm rewards diversity and relevance.

The "Four Peaks" Theory & Marketing Moments

[00:32:20] - Purpose of Marketing Moments:

These moments are designed to create cash flow during otherwise "non-interesting" times of the year.

[00:33:00] - Identifying a "Peak":

Instead of forcing a fit (like Mother's Day for a non-gifting brand), brands should align with existing cultural moments or events that match their specific brand ethos and audience.

[00:36:00] - "Catching the Wave":

Richard adds a metaphor that finding marketing moments is like surfing; it's about catching an existing wave (cultural trend) rather than trying to manufacture one.

Discounting & Offer Strategy

[00:49:15] - Subscription vs. Multi-SKU:

For multi-SKU apparel brands, frequent discounting risks "deal habitual behavior".

[00:49:40] - Membership Model Benefits:

For membership or recurring revenue businesses, aggressive constant discounting (e.g., 70% off) is often appropriate because the primary goal is capturing the customer for life.
