BFCM 2025 Key Trends (6 min)

Recent Trainings

In this clip, Taylor lays out the key trends to consider for BFCM 2025.

[00:00:00 – 00:01:20] Market Environment and Pricing Challenges

Inflation is driving consumer price sensitivity and softer demand.

Brands face higher COGS due to duties and costs hitting inventory.

Likely outcome: heavy discounting but still elevated prices, creating consumer friction.

Competitive positioning and gross margin strength will matter.

[00:01:40 – 00:02:27] AppLovin as a Scalable Q4 Channel

AppLovin emerged last year as a major media channel for gifting-oriented customers.

Platform reopens to advertisers on October 1 (still managed service).

Anticipation of significant spend increase in that channel; brands should prepare tests early.

[00:02:27 – 00:03:27] Black Friday / Cyber Monday Playbook

Key focus: understand how your brand participates in BFCM and customer value during the moment.

For many, this period is the most efficient new customer acquisition opportunity.

New customers typically show higher AOV, lower LTV.

Best practice: tiered offer rollouts—VIPs first, then existing customers, then general public.

[00:03:49 – 00:04:25] Measurement and Attribution Nuances

Leading weeks into BFCM show longer attribution windows (1-day, 7-day, 28-day).

Early November spending often looks inefficient until later conversions roll in.

Adjust targets accordingly for that lag.

[00:04:06 – 00:05:31] Calendar and Year-Over-Year Comparisons

This year’s BFCM calendar splits November/December differently than last year.

In 2023: 3 days November, 2 days December.

In 2024: 4 days November, 1 day December.

This affects forecasting and YoY comps—November may look inflated, December depressed.

Calendar shifts will cause even bigger distortions next year.
