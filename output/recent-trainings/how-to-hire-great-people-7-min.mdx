How to Hire Great People (7 min)

Recent Trainings

In this clip, Taylor gives advice on hiring the best people for your marketing team.

Timestamps:

[00:00:00 – 00:01:00] The Pitfalls of Early Hiring

Many founders offload low-value tasks just to free up time.

This creates cost centers rather than generating real business growth.

[00:01:00 – 00:02:00] Inverse Hiring Philosophy

Instead of outsourcing low-value work, focus on hiring for the

highest-value tasks

.

Example: hiring someone to improve Meta ads rather than manage organic social.

[00:02:00 – 00:03:00] Finding the Right Talent

Look for someone who can independently create ads (e.g., with just an iPhone).

Test candidates with practical challenges (e.g., “make 10 ads in an hour”).

The goal is to find a high-capacity person motivated by opportunity.

[00:03:00 – 00:04:00] Agency Partnerships as an Alternative

Agencies sometimes offer creative work for a percentage of ad spend (no upfront cost).

This allows businesses to access creative resources with lower financial risk.

[00:04:00 – 00:05:30] ROI-Driven Hiring Framework

Every role should have a potential

10x ROI pathway

.

Better to take risks on high-return hires than spend limited resources on cost-heavy roles.

Three approaches:

Agencies with performance-based models.

Hiring creators to produce high-volume ad content.

Turning organic or retention channels (social, email) into revenue drivers.

[00:05:30 – 00:07:00] Rethinking Job Descriptions and Recruitment

Job postings rarely attract top talent, especially for small businesses.

High-quality hires usually come through networks, referrals, or direct outreach.

Businesses should rely on relational equity and compelling opportunities to win great candidates.
